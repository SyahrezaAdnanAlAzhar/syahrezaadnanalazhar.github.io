{/* SECTION 4 — Technical Architecture */}
{/* ========================================================================= */}

<Section title="Technical Architecture" defaultOpen={false}>
  <P>
    <B>The system combines semantic segmentation and monocular depth estimation to estimate food volume from a single top-down image</B>. Segmentation masks are fused with calibrated depth maps to compute food volume, which is then mapped to nutritional values using Ministry of Health Standards, and WHO Child Growth Standards. The applied Ministry of Health standards include the Nutritional Adequacy Rate (Angka Kecukupan Gizi - AKG), the Indonesian Food Composition Table (Tabel Komposisi Pangan Indonesia - TKPI), and the Food Exchange List (Daftar Bahan Makanan Penukar).
  </P>
</Section>

{/* ========================================================================= */}
{/* SECTION 5 — Key Technical Decisions */}
{/* ========================================================================= */}

<Section title="Key Technical Decisions" defaultOpen={true}>
  <ul class="custom-list">
    <li>Chose DeepLabV3 for stable boundary segmentation on food textures</li>
    <li>Used MiDaS v3.1 to avoid hardware dependency (no depth sensor)</li>
    <li>
      Applied plate-diameter calibration (25 cm) to convert relative depth into
      metric volume
    </li>
    <li>
      Designed system to work offline-first for low-connectivity environments
    </li>
  </ul>
</Section>
